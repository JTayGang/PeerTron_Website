Why Should You Care About Undervolting?

Undervolting isn’t just a geeky trick—it saves money, improves efficiency, and extends hardware lifespan. Here’s how:

  • Lower Temperatures: Less voltage means less heat, improving cooling.
  • Energy Savings: Cut power usage, reducing electricity costs.
  • Quieter Fans: Lower temps mean fans spin slower and make less noise.
  • More Performance: Some systems maintain or improve speeds when undervolted.

One online news outlet reduced GPU power draw by 40% with only single digit performance impact.

Real-World Impact

A typical gaming PC can save 50-150 watts of power when undervolted. Over a year, that can add up to noticeable savings.

Example: If electricity costs $0.15 per kWh, and you save 50W per hour over 5 hours a day, that’s around $15-$45 a year—just for one PC! The AC bill won't be as high either, as less heat means a cooler room.
