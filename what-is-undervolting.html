What is Undervolting?

Modern computer hardware is built with more voltage than needed. By lowering voltage, you can make your system more power-efficient while maintaining the same or slightly better levels of performance.

An example from one online user that displays major power savings with minimal performance impact.

Why does this happen?

Manufacturers provide more electricity than needed to ensure the same level of stability and performance across their products. Most computer hardware is above this manufacturer tolerance, and by tuning voltage levels you can optimize your hardware for better efficiency.
